orangemitts.jpg

  • craft: knitting
  • pattern/reference: Miss Fox (free on DROPS Design)
  • yarn: DROPS Alpaca (sport weight alpaca wool)

i don't wanna be too hard on myself, but for the sake of transparency i must admit that these arent up to my usual standard. i must have messed up the math somewhere, but i'm not sure where. i tried my best to correct it, but they still came out pretty mismatched

i also kept making small annoying mistakes that forced me to frog it repeatedly. overall i did not find making these to be a fun experience

on the bright side, this pattern did teach me a new method for avoiding gaps on the gusset increases, which I'll include here for reference:

  1. Row 1: increase via yo on each side of thumb
  2. Row 2: as you knit around, twist yo st before knitting it

i've already started knitting a new pair of mittens. hopefully i will redeem myself

dogscarrf.png

  • craft: knitting
  • pattern/reference: n/a
  • yarn: Loops & Threads Chenille Home Slim (super bulky acrylic)

i made this for my sisters dog during the brief time it rained during a camping trip. not to brag but she loved it! and i got rid of some of this chenille yarn thats been haunting my stash, so ultimately the rain was a win for both of us

i cant give an accurate description of how i made it, but it went something like this:

  1. 1. CO 44 sts on US 10 / 6mm needles (or adjust size as needed). join in round
  2. 2. 2x2 rib for a while. measure with your heart
  3. 3. BO 22 sts
  4. 4. from this point on, repeat:
    1. i. k1, k2tog, k to last 3 sts, ssk, k1
    2. ii. knit across
  5. 5. BO once there arent enough sts left to repeat
  6. 6. give to dog. wait for rain to end
