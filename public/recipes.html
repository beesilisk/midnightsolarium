GRANDMA'S ZUCCHINI SALSA ★

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 cups zucchini, chopped
  • 1 cup onion, chopped
  • 1/2 red pepper, chopped
  • 1/2 green pepper, chopped
  • 1 jalapeno (or to taste), chopped
  • 1 tbsp salt


  • 2 cups chopped tomatoes
  • 5.5 oz. can tomato pasta
  • 1/2 tsp dry mustard
  • 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 tsp cumin
  • 1/2 cup white vinegar
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp chili powder
  • 2 tsp cornstarch

INSTRUCTIONS

Put zucchini, onion, red & green pepper, jalapeno, and salt in dutch oven. Mix together and leave in refrigerator overnight.

Next morning, DON'T DRAIN! Add the rest of the ingredients and mix well. Boil for 10 minutes, then simmer for an hour. Chill. Makes about 1 litre.



If you want to make enough for multiple jars (and you know how to jar food safely), quadruple the recipe.

POPPYCOCK ★

INGREDIENTS

  • 24 cups fresh popped popcorn (unsalted, unbuttered)
  • 2 2/3 cups brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 cup margarine
  • 1 cup corn syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla (or more if you like)
  • nuts, roasted & unsalted - can be almonds, pecans, hazelnuts, and/or brazils (NOT walnuts!)
  • Optional: some people add cherries, raisins, or craisins
    • This recipe is very customizable. Add whatever your heart desires

INSTRUCTIONS

(This is much easier to make with 2 people)

Spray big stainless steel bowl with Pam to avoid sticking. Place popcorn & nuts in bowl.

Combine sugar, corn syrup, and margarine in large pan over low heat. Stir well while it melts together, slowly raising the heat until it forms a hard ball. Remove from heat and add vanilla and stir very well.

Pour over popcorn and nuts and mix well. Spread over greased baking sheets. Let cool and dry.

Makes about 3 ice cream pails full.

  • 8oz swiss or mozzarella cheese, sliced

KRUMPRA & KNADLE ★

INGREDIENTS

  • 6 potatoes, cut into small pieces (not too small, or they'll turn to mush)
  • onion, to taste
  • 2 tbsp butter
  • paprika, to taste
  • water


  • 2 cups flour
  • salt & pepper
  • water


  • 1/2 cup shredded medium cheddar
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt

IPREN

(potato soup)

Sauté onions in 2 tablespoons of butter. Sprinkle with paprika, and add enough water to cover potatoes.

Season with salt, and boil until potatoes are tender.

KNADLES

(bread dumplings)

Combine flour, salt & pepper, and enough water to make hard dough. Mix well.

Flatten pieces of dough. They should be about as wide as a loonie (approx. 1") and about 1/8" thick.

When potato soup is ready, "pluck" knadle dough into soup. Cook 10-15 minutes. Add cheddar and yogurt. Eat! Enjoy!

APPLE CRISP ♥

INGREDIENTS

  • 10 cups apples, peeled cored & sliced (about 10-12 apples)
  • 1 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tbsp flour
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/2 cup water


  • 1 cup quick-cooking oats
  • 1 cup flour
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 cup butter, melted

INSTRUCTIONS

Place apple slices in 9"×13" pan. In a bowl, mix the granulated sugar, 1 tbsp flour, and ground cinnamon together, and sprinkle over the apples. Then pour water evenly over the apples.

In a bowl, combine oats, 1 cup flour, brown sugar, baking soda, baking powder, and butter together into a crumbly mixture. Evenly layer on top of apples.

Bake at 350°F for about 45 minutes.

(the size of the apple slices is up to personal taste. large slices mean less work. i prefer to cut them thinner - but any piece under 1/4" thick risks turning to mush)

BANANA SCONES

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 banana, ripe or overripe
  • 1 egg
  • 1/3 cup heavy cream (i usually cheat and use half&half)
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 cups flour
  • 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 2 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 cup walnuts
  • sugar for sprinkling

INSTRUCTIONS

Preheat oven to 400°F.

Mash banana until fairly smooth. Add mashed banana (should be around 1/2 cup) to a 2 cup measuring cup. Add the egg to the cup and whisk together until well combined.

Add enough cream to reach the 1 cup measuring line. Add vanilla and stir. Place cup in freezer for 10 minutes.

Place butter in a bowl and microwave for 50-60 seconds, or until just barely melted. Set aside to cool.

In another bowl, combine flour, brown sugar, baking powder, salt and walnuts. Make well in center.

Remove cream from freezer and add butter, stirring until mixture is lumpy.

Pour banana mixture into the well in the flour bowl. Stir from the bottom up with a sturdy spatula or large spoon until flour is mixed in. The dough should be a bit crumbly and stiff.

IT'S KNEADING TIME! Place the dough on a lightly floured surface. And knead 2-3 times. Then pat it into a circle that's about 6" diameter.

Using a knife, cut the circle into 8 equal wedges.

(if you like, this is a good time to wrap and freeze the scone if you'd rather bake later)

Place wedges on pan lined with parchment paper. Brush tops with cream and sprinkle with sugar.

Place scones in oven and immediately reduce heat to 375°F. Bake for 20-25 minutes, until scones are golden brown. A toothpick inserted in the center should come out clean.

CHOCOLATE BANANA CAKE

  • 2 eggs
  • 3-4 very ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/3 cup sour cream
  • 2 tsp baking soda
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2 1/4 cup flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/3 cup cocoa powder
  • 1 cup boiling water

INSTRUCTIONS

Add baking soda to sour cream; let foam while making rest of cake

COFFEE CAKE

INGREDIENTS

  • 1/4 cup flour
  • 2/3 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 cup butter


  • 2 cups flour
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 1 egg
  • approx. 3/4 cup milk
  • 1 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

STREUSEL TOPPING

In a medium bowl, combine 1/4 cup flour, 2/3 cup granulated sugar, and cinnamon. Cut in the 1/4 cup butter until crumbly mixture is formed.

CAKE

Preheat oven to 350°F and grease 9"×13" pan.

In large bowl, combine 2 cups flour, 3/4 cups sugar, baking powder, and salt. Cut in 1/2 cup butter. The mixture should look crumbly.

Crack the egg into your measuring cup. Then use milk to fill it to 1 cup. Stir in vanilla. Pour all this into the large bowl and mix only until moistened.

Spread cake batter into pan. Sprinkle streusel overtop. Bake for 20-25 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted at the center comes out clean.

THE MOST SOFT & CHEWY CHOCOLATE CHIP COOKIES ♥

INGREDIENTS

  • 3/4 cup unsalted butter, softened
  • 1/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 3/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 egg
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • 2 cups flour
  • 2 tsp cornstarch
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup chocolate chips (or to taste)

INSTRUCTIONS

Beat butter and both sugars together until creamy (approx. 2 minutes). Beat in egg and vanilla.

In separate bowl whisk flour, cornstarch, baking soda, and salt. Add to wet ingredients, beating until combined. Fold in the chocolate chips.

CHILL THE DOUGH!!! For at least 1 hour in the fridge, but preferably 3 or more. You can leave it to chill for up to 72 hours, but make sure the dough is covered.

Preheat oven 350°F. Line baking sheets with parchment paper.

Roll dough into 1 heaping tablespoon balls. Place each 3" apart on baking sheets.

Bake for 11-12 minutes, until barely golden on edges. They will look underbaked on top. This is normal.

Cool cookies on baking sheet for 5 minutes, then transfer to cooling rack.

makes about 30 cookies. stays fresh for about a week if kept covered at room temp

PUMPKIN SPICE SNICKERDOODLES ♥

COOKIE INGREDIENTS

  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 cup brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 2 whole eggs + 1 yolk
  • 2 tsp vanilla
  • 3 tsp pumpkin spice*
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 3/4 tsp salt
  • 3 cups flour

COATING

  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp pumpkin spice*

*PUMPKIN SPICE

  • 4 tsp ground cinnamon
  • 1 tsp ground nutmeg
  • 1 tsp ground ginger
  • 1/2 tsp ground cloves
    • (you may need to make more if you run out of coating)

INSTRUCTIONS

Cream butter and both sugars together for 2 minutes until light and fluffy. Add both eggs and additional yolk into butter along with vanilla and beat until smooth.

Mix in 3 tsp pumpkin spice, baking powder, and salt. Reduce speed on mixer to low and mix in flour.

Chill dough for at least an hour.

Line baking sheet with parchment paper and preheat oven to 375°F.

Mix coating ingredients together in a bowl. Roll dough into large balls, approx. 1 1/2" diameter. Then roll the balls in coating, covering completely.

Bake for 15ish minutes until cookies are lightly browned at the bottom. They will look very puffy. Remove from oven and transfer to cooling rack.

Makes about 30 cookies.

TOO MANY BRAN MUFFINS ★

INGREDIENTS

  • 4 cups bran flakes cereal
  • 2 cups all bran cereal
  • 2 cups boiling water


  • 1 cup butter
  • 1 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 1/2 cup brown sugar
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 cups buttermilk


  • 5 cups flour
  • 2 tbsp baking soda
  • 1 tbsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 2 cups raisins (optional)

INSTRUCTIONS

In large bowl, combine cereals and boiling water. Let stand.

In separate bowl cream butter and sugars. Beat in eggs one at a time. Mix in buttermilk. Stir in cereal mix.

In another bowl combine flour, soda, powder, salt, and raisins. Mix well. Add to batter, and stir to combine. Store in fridge.

Fill muffin cups 3/4 full. Bake at 400°F for 20-25 minutes. Remove from pan after 5 minutes.

Makes a LOT of muffins. I'm not kidding. You might wanna keep the batter covered in the fridge and bake in small batches. The batter should keep for about 6 weeks.
If you choose to bake them all at once, be prepared to gift some to your friends and loved ones. Or at least put some in the freezer.

DANDELION TEA

INGREDIENTS

  • 8 oz. boiling water
  • 1/2 cup fresh dandelion petals
    • (pull the petals out by hand, or cut them off the head. it's fine to leave some green bits, but leaving the head will make the tea too bitter)

INSTRUCTIONS

Rinse the petals off before making the tea.

Place the petals in tea ball and steep for 10 minutes. The tea will taste mild and light.

Goes well with honey. You can also add lemon, mint, raspberry leaves, and/or chamomile.
